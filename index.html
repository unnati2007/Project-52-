Panda

  1. A giant panda is much bigger than your teddy bear.
  2. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  3. Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.
  4. Pandas have 6 toes to grasp bamboo.
  5. Newborn pandas are pink!

Teddy Bear

  1. The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
  2. The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall!
  3. The world's largest stitched teddy bear is a mere 55 foot tall!
  4. Winnie the Pooh was based on a real bear

Chameleon

  1. Chameleons take a while to hatch, and don’t live very long
  2. Chameleons come in a lot of different shapes and sizes
  3. Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues
  4. Chameleons are not deaf but they do not actually have ear openings.
  5. Species of chameleon can be as small as 15 mm (0.59 in) or as large as 69 cm (27 in).

Lion

  1. Lions are the only cats that live in groups.
  2. Female lions are the main hunters.
  3. A lion's roar can be heard up to eight kilometres away.
  4. A lion can run for short distances at 50 mph and leap as far as 36 feet.
  5. A lion may sleep up to 20 hours a day.

Pelican

  1. A pelican can hold up to three gallons of water in its pouch.
  2. Pelicans are splendid fliers
  3. They are social birds and typically travel in flocks, often strung out in a line.
  4. Many pelicans fish by swimming in cooperative groups.
  5. Both females and males contribute to the making of the nest.

Giraffe

  1. Giraffes are the tallest mammals on Earth.
  2. Giraffes only need to drink once every few days
  3. Giraffes are very social animals and roam around in groups.
  4. In the wild these beautiful creatures live for around 25 years.
  5. Giraffes are between 16 and 20 ft (4.8–6 m) tall.
